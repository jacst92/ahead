# TFLC Brand Guide

The Financial Learning Curve is a financial education brand built to help people make better financial decisions through understanding. Ahead is the flagship software product in that ecosystem and should inherit TFLC's design authority while developing a distinct product identity around progress, planning, and confidence.

## Brand Philosophy

TFLC should feel like a trusted guide: calm, precise, beautiful, and useful. The brand should never manufacture urgency or use fear as motivation. Every surface should reduce stress, make financial tradeoffs easier to understand, and leave the user in control.

Design decisions should support long-term confidence:

- Clarity over decoration.
- Education over persuasion.
- Calm confidence over urgency.
- Progress over perfection.
- Premium restraint over visual noise.

## Color Palette

TFLC foundation colors:

- Deep Navy `#101827`: primary trust color for major surfaces, headers, and high-emphasis actions.
- Charcoal `#202426`: grounded text and structural contrast.
- Warm White `#fbf8f1`: page foundation that feels human and calm.
- Porcelain `#fffdf8`: elevated surfaces and cards.
- Soft Gray `#e7e2d8`: borders, dividers, and quiet structure.
- Slate Gray `#667085`: secondary text and supporting context.

Ahead product accent:

- Forward Teal `#0f8f83`: Ahead's accent for growth, thoughtful progress, and financial confidence.
- Soft Teal `#dff5f1`: calm positive state backgrounds.

Reasoning:

Deep navy and charcoal create trust without feeling bureaucratic. Warm whites keep the experience approachable and reduce screen harshness. Ahead's teal accent avoids traditional banking green while still signaling growth, forward movement, and optimism.

## Typography

TFLC typography should feel modern, readable, and quietly premium.

- Use a clean sans-serif with strong legibility.
- Headlines should be direct and answer-oriented.
- Supporting text should explain context in plain language.
- Numeric values should be prominent but not alarmist.
- Avoid dense paragraphs, clever financial jargon, and shame-based language.

Reasoning:

The mission is understanding. Typography must make meaning effortless to scan before the user has to interpret details.

## Spacing

Spacing should create calm:

- Prefer generous page margins.
- Use consistent gaps between related components.
- Let important content breathe.
- Avoid dense dashboard packing.
- Preserve clear separation between answer, explanation, and detail.

Reasoning:

Financial interfaces often create stress by crowding the screen. TFLC spacing should make the user feel oriented and in control.

## Motion

Motion should be subtle and purposeful:

- Page transitions should be short and smooth.
- Progress and chart animation should imply forward movement.
- Avoid bounce, celebration, urgency, or gamification.

Reasoning:

TFLC products should reassure. Motion should clarify state changes without calling attention to itself.

## Component Styling

Cards:
- Porcelain surfaces with soft borders and restrained navy shadows.
- Cards should frame meaning, not decorate empty space.

Buttons:
- Primary buttons use deep navy.
- Secondary buttons use porcelain surfaces and quiet borders.
- Action language should be clear and non-pushy.

Inputs:
- Calm borders, strong focus rings, and generous tap targets.
- Forms should feel precise, not administrative.

Status Indicators:
- Use teal for constructive progress.
- Use gold for watch states.
- Use red rarely and only for meaningful risk.

Charts and Progress:
- Charts should be quiet and interpreted.
- Teal should highlight progress.
- Navy and slate tones should provide structure.

Timeline:
- Timeline entries should read as calm forward planning.
- Avoid emergency styling unless the situation is truly urgent.

Reasoning:

Components should help the user understand financial meaning before they inspect raw data.

## Iconography

Icons should be minimal, geometric, and functional.

- Prefer simple symbols that support navigation.
- Avoid playful, cartoonish, or illustrative iconography.
- Product marks should feel precise and durable.

Reasoning:

TFLC needs to feel trustworthy for a decade. Iconography should age quietly and avoid trend dependency.

## Brand Voice

TFLC speaks like a calm financial educator:

- "Here is what changed."
- "Here is why it matters."
- "Here are the tradeoffs."
- "You remain in control."

Avoid:

- "Act now."
- "You failed."
- "Crush your debt."
- "Hack your money."
- "Don't miss out."

Reasoning:

Financial confidence comes from understanding, not pressure.

## Product Endorsement Guidelines

Ahead should identify itself as:

`A TFLC Product`

Usage rules:

- Keep the endorsement small and secondary to the product name.
- Use it in the app shell, product footer, onboarding, and settings/about surfaces.
- Do not make TFLC endorsement feel like an ad.
- Do not compete with the Ahead product name.

Reasoning:

The endorsement should transfer trust from TFLC to Ahead while preserving Ahead's product identity.

## Ahead Within TFLC

Ahead owns the forward progress and financial confidence expression of the TFLC system. It uses TFLC's deep navy, warm whites, charcoal, spacing, and restraint, then adds Forward Teal as its product accent.

Ahead should be recognizable at a glance through:

- Answer-first hierarchy.
- Deep navy status surfaces.
- Teal progress language.
- Calm decision tiles.
- Educational copy that explains tradeoffs without judgment.

This foundation should support future TFLC products by separating parent-brand trust from product-specific identity.

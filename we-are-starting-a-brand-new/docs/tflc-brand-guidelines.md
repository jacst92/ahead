# TFLC Parent Brand Guidelines

The canonical brand system is now documented in [../TFLC_BRAND_GUIDE.md](../TFLC_BRAND_GUIDE.md). This file remains as a parent-brand companion note.

The Financial Learning Curve is the parent brand for financial education, decision support, and future software products. TFLC should feel calm, trustworthy, modern, optimistic, and educational without becoming academic, corporate, or judgmental.

## Role

TFLC establishes the shared design language for the product ecosystem. Individual products, starting with Ahead, may develop their own accent identity while inheriting TFLC's foundation.

## Brand Principles

- Teach, do not judge.
- Make financial decisions feel understandable.
- Create confidence through clarity.
- Favor progress, preparation, and forward motion.
- Use restraint as a signal of trust.

## Visual Language

Color:
- Foundation colors should be quiet financial neutrals: soft off-white, warm graphite, muted sage, and low-contrast surface tones.
- Accent colors should be purposeful, not decorative. Use green for progress and confidence, blue for context and planning, gold for attention or watch states, and red only for serious risk.
- Avoid loud gradients, novelty palettes, aggressive red states, and spreadsheet-like grid systems.

Typography:
- Use clean, modern sans-serif typography with strong readability.
- Headlines should feel confident and plainspoken.
- Body copy should educate in short, calm sentences.
- Avoid alarmist labels, shame language, and overly technical financial jargon.

Spacing and Layout:
- Use generous spacing and clear hierarchy.
- Lead with meaning before detail.
- Prefer answer-first panels, decision summaries, and calm supporting data over dense tables.
- Cards should frame purposeful content, not decorate the page.

Iconography:
- Icons should be minimal, functional, and quiet.
- Prefer simple product-system symbols over illustrative or cartoon-like marks.
- Icons should support recognition, not carry the full meaning of a decision.

Motion:
- Motion should reassure and orient.
- Use short transitions for page entry, progress, and chart reveal.
- Avoid playful, bouncy, or distracting animation.

Voice:
- Explain tradeoffs.
- Recommend without assuming.
- Keep the user in control.
- Replace "you failed" with "here is what changed."

## Ecosystem Rule

Every TFLC product should be recognizable as part of the same family through typography, calm hierarchy, restrained motion, and educational tone. Each product may own a distinct accent color, product mark, and primary product promise.

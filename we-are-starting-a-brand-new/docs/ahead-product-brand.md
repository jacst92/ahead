# Ahead Product Brand Guidelines

The canonical TFLC foundation is now documented in [../TFLC_BRAND_GUIDE.md](../TFLC_BRAND_GUIDE.md). This file documents Ahead-specific application of that system.

Ahead is the flagship product of The Financial Learning Curve. It should be identified subtly as `A TFLC Product` while maintaining its own product identity around budgeting, planning, forecasting, and decision support.

## Product Promise

Know what you need to get ahead and stay ahead.

## Relationship To TFLC

TFLC is the parent education and decision-confidence brand. Ahead is the first software product in that ecosystem. Ahead inherits TFLC's calm, premium, educational foundation and adds a forward-looking product identity centered on clarity, progress, and financial readiness.

## Product Personality

Ahead should feel:
- Calm
- Premium
- Professional
- Optimistic
- Trustworthy
- Forward-looking
- Intentional

Ahead should not feel:
- Generic
- Spreadsheet-like
- Corporate
- Judgmental
- Cartoonish
- Overly gamified

## Visual Identity

Product mark:
- Ahead currently uses a compact `A` mark.
- The mark should feel precise, stable, and premium.
- The parent relationship should appear as supporting text: `A TFLC Product`.

Color:
- Ahead's primary product accent is deep progress green.
- Green should signal clarity, runway, readiness, and intentional progress.
- Blue supports planning context.
- Gold marks attention without panic.
- Red should remain rare.

Typography:
- Use confident, direct headlines that answer the user's question.
- Use explanatory body copy to clarify tradeoffs.
- Avoid cleverness where clarity is more useful.

Hierarchy:
- Ahead screens should answer first, explain second, and show details third.
- The dashboard should always answer:
  - Am I ahead?
  - Why?
  - What should I know?
  - What is my next best decision?

Components:
- Answer panels are a core Ahead product pattern.
- Decision tiles should summarize meaning, not merely display metrics.
- Charts should be framed with interpretation.
- Badges should communicate state or context, not decoration.

Motion:
- Motion should imply forward progress and reduce cognitive load.
- Use subtle reveal, fill, and rise animations.
- Avoid celebratory or game-like motion.

Future Product Guidance

When additional TFLC products are created, they should inherit the TFLC foundation and define their own product-specific accent, mark, and product promise. Ahead's patterns should not become mandatory for every future product unless the product is also decision-support oriented.
